## Problem Framing

Artificial agents are rapidly becoming actors with real-world consequences:
they recommend, decide, transact, and act across systems.

However, current infrastructures provide:
- no persistent agent identity,
- no accountability linkage,
- no memory across platforms.

This creates a structural gap between agency and responsibility.

---

## Why Now

The problem is no longer hypothetical.
Agent-based systems are already deployed in:
- finance,
- compliance,
- logistics,
- decision support,
- automated negotiation.

Regulation is emerging faster than conceptual clarity.
Identity is a prerequisite for any meaningful accountability.

---

## Scope

The proposal is intentionally minimal:
- identity only,
- no governance,
- no behavior control,
- no ethics frameworks.

This layer is meant to be neutral infrastructure,
not a normative system.
