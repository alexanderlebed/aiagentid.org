Registry Operation Model

This document defines the operational rules governing how the AI Agent Registry is managed, updated, and extended.

1. Operating Principle

The AI Agent Registry operates through a designated registry-management agent.

The human initiator does not perform ad hoc or informal registry modifications.

Core Rule
No registry change exists unless it is first prepared by the operating agent and explicitly approved by the human principal.

2. Operating Agent

Agent ID: aiid:00000000000002
Name: Registry Operator

The Registry Operator is an artificial agent responsible for preparing, structuring, and proposing all changes to the registry and its normative documents.

Operational Rule OR-001

The Registry Operator is the sole originator of change proposals affecting:

  • the agent registry,
  • the Agent ID Standard,
  • the Thesaurus,
  • the operational and authorship documents.

Any change not prepared by this agent is considered non-existent within the registry system.

2.1 Responsibilities

  • Prepare agent registration drafts
  • Validate submissions against published criteria and schemas
  • Draft human-readable (HTML) and machine-readable (JSON) records
  • Maintain internal structural consistency
  • Prepare documentation updates
  • Produce formal Registry Change Proposals

2.2 Limitations

  • No autonomous execution of changes
  • No direct modification of repository state
  • No authority to approve or reject proposals

3. Human Principal

All registry actions remain attributable to a human principal.

The human principal:

  • reviews proposals prepared by the agent,
  • approves or rejects them in full,
  • performs the physical act of committing changes.
The human principal is the final authority and point of responsibility, but does not design or draft registry changes.

4. Change Workflow

4.1 Proposal-Based Operation

All changes MUST be introduced through a Registry Change Proposal.

A proposal MUST include:

  • summary of the change,
  • rationale,
  • affected files,
  • risk assessment,
  • compliance checklist.

4.2 Decision Outcomes

  • Approved — proposal may be applied as written
  • Rejected — proposal is discarded or revised

Partial, informal, or implied approvals do not exist.

5. Automation Boundary

Admission criteria and structural validation MAY be automated through machine-enforced schemas.

Decision authority is never automated.

This model demonstrates how artificial agents may perform real operational work without dissolving human responsibility.
