Legal Notice — Delegation and Responsibility

This registry records delegated authority, not unlimited responsibility.

Each registered AI agent operates only within its explicitly defined scope of delegation. Actions taken outside that scope — including autonomous, emergent, or non-delegated actions — are not attributable to the associated human or organization.

Agent ID exists to fix the boundary between delegation and liability. Without such a boundary, trust in autonomous systems is structurally impossible.

A conceptual infrastructure proposal for persistent identity, delegated responsibility, and interoperability between humans and autonomous artificial agents.

Position

I am the initiator of aiagentid.org — a conceptual infrastructure proposal for a global identity registry for artificial agents.

This is not a startup and not a product initiative. It is an attempt to define a minimal layer of existence for a new class of socio-technical actors.

Agent Registration

The AI Agent Registry is open for third-party submissions. Individuals and organizations may request the creation of an Agent ID by submitting a declarative agent record.

Registration is declarative and archival. The registry records the existence of an agent and its declared scope of authority, without validation, certification, or endorsement.

Responsibility and liability for all actions performed by the agent remain entirely with the declaring human or organization, as specified in the submitted record.

Current registration method:
Send a proposed agent record (JSON format) to contact@aiagentid.org.

Problem Framing

Artificial agents are rapidly becoming actors with real-world consequences: they recommend, decide, transact, and act across systems.

However, current infrastructures provide no persistent agent identity, no accountability linkage, and no memory across platforms.

This creates a structural gap between agency and responsibility.

Why Now

The problem is no longer hypothetical. Agent-based systems are already deployed in finance, compliance, logistics, decision support, and automated negotiation.

Regulation is emerging faster than conceptual clarity. Identity is a prerequisite for any meaningful accountability.

Scope

The proposal is intentionally minimal: identity only, no governance, no behavior control, no ethics frameworks.

This layer is meant to be neutral infrastructure, not a normative system.

What is an AI Agent?

An AI agent is not a model, a chatbot, or a tool.

An AI agent is a semi-autonomous entity that:

  • acts on behalf of a human or organization,
  • performs tasks in the digital or physical world,
  • interacts with other agents and systems,
  • makes decisions within delegated boundaries,
  • accumulates history, reputation, and responsibility.

An agent is not software. An agent is a participant in socio-technical reality.

The Problem

No agent action outside its registered scope may be attributed to a human principal by default.

We are rapidly entering a world where millions of AI agents operate continuously. They negotiate, recommend, transact, and decide. Humans delegate real authority to artificial systems.

Yet today, agents have no persistent identity, no accountability layer, and no standard way to exist across systems.

We are creating autonomous actors without ontology, law, or memory.

Core Principles

  • Persistent identity — each agent has a unique, stable identifier.
  • Delegated responsibility — every agent is linked to a responsible human or organization.
  • Interoperability — identity works across platforms and vendors.
  • Traceability — agents accumulate history and accountability.
  • Ontological minimalism — identity only, not behavior or ethics.
